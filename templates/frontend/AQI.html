AQI Detail Information

Air Quality Index (AQI) forecasting involves analyzing air pollution levels and predicting trends to help the public avoid health risks from poor air quality. AQI is measured on a scale from 0 to 500, with higher values indicating worse air quality. Hereâ€™s a detailed breakdown of the sensitive and critical aspects of AQI forecasting

Health Impact Details by AQI Range:-

* 0-50 (Good): Air quality is considered satisfactory, with little or no risk to health.
* 51-100 (Moderate): Air quality is acceptable; however, some pollutants may pose moderate health concerns for sensitive individuals.
* 101-150 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups): Members of sensitive groups (e.g., children, elderly, people with respiratory issues) may experience health effects.
* 151-200 (Unhealthy): Everyone may start to experience adverse health effects, with sensitive individuals being at higher risk.
* 201-300 (Very Unhealthy): Health alerts indicate that everyone may experience serious health effects.
* 301-500 (Hazardous): Serious health effects are possible for the entire population, often triggering health warnings and emergency responses.
